## 📌 Executive Summary
This repository contains a Tier-1 Market Risk model and its corresponding Model Validation framework, designed to estimate **97.5% Expected Shortfall (ES)** for a multi-asset portfolio. 

Driven by the regulatory shift from Value-at-Risk (VaR) to Expected Shortfall under the Basel Committee's **Fundamental Review of the Trading Book (FRTB)**, this project addresses the mathematical challenges of capturing joint extreme tail events (systemic crashes) and validating non-elicitable risk metrics. The methodology relies on an ARMA-GJR-GARCH marginal filtering process coupled with a Multivariate Student-t Copula, followed by an Acerbi-Székely backtesting framework.

## 🏛 Regulatory Context & Rationale
Under previous Basel accords, VaR was the standard risk metric. However, VaR is not a *coherent risk measure* (it lacks sub-additivity) and ignores the severity of losses beyond the threshold. FRTB mandates the use of Expected Shortfall. 
Because ES cannot be directly backtested using standard unconditional coverage tests (like Kupiec POF), advanced conditional magnitude tests are required for Model Risk Validation (MRV) compliance.

## 🔬 Methodology & Pipeline

The modeling pipeline consists of 5 distinct quantitative phases:

### 1. Multivariate Portfolio Construction
A dynamically weighted 5-asset portfolio representing diverse market risk factors: Broad US Equities (SPY), Emerging Markets (EEM), Long-Duration Treasuries (TLT), Gold (GLD), and High-Volatility Tech (NVDA).

### 2. Marginal Modeling (Volatility & Asymmetry)
To capture the stylized facts of financial time series (volatility clustering and the leverage effect), each asset's log-returns are modeled using a **GJR-GARCH(1,1)** process with a **Skewed Student-t distribution**.
* Standardized residuals are extracted and mapped to a Uniform(0,1) space via the Probability Integral Transform (PIT) using an Empirical Cumulative Distribution Function (ECDF).
* **Validation Check:** Kolmogorov-Smirnov (KS) tests confirm the strict uniformity of the transformed marginals.

### 3. Multivariate Dependence (Tail Risk)
A **Multivariate Student-t Copula** is calibrated to the uniform pseudo-observations using Kendall's $\tau$ rank correlation. Unlike Gaussian Copulas, the t-Copula explicitly models heavy tail dependence, allowing the system to capture extreme joint market crashes.

### 4. Monte Carlo Risk Simulation
10,000 $T+1$ (1-day ahead) scenarios are generated by sampling from the fitted Copula, inverting the PIT, and re-introducing the conditional volatility forecasts.
* **Metrics Extracted:** 97.5% VaR and 97.5% Expected Shortfall.

### 5. Model Risk Validation (Acerbi-Székely Framework)
The model is subjected to rigorous historical backtesting over a 10-year out-of-sample window. 
* **Unconditional Coverage:** Validating baseline VaR breach frequencies.
* **Conditional Magnitude:** Implementing the Acerbi-Székely test statistic ($Z$) to validate the magnitude of the Expected Shortfall predictions against actual realized tail losses.

$$Z = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{t=1}^{T} \frac{r_t \cdot I(r_t < VaR_t)}{ES_t} + 1$$

## 📊 Key Results & Validation Metrics

The model demonstrated exceptional accuracy in both frequency and magnitude of tail losses.

* **1-Day 97.5% VaR:** $-2.63\%$
* **1-Day 97.5% ES:** $-3.95\%$
* **Unconditional Coverage (VaR):** PASS (62 actual breaches vs. 62.9 expected)
* **Acerbi-Székely Test (ES Magnitude):** PASS 
* **Z-Statistic:** `0.0767` *(A value near 0 indicates the realized loss magnitude matched the model's Expected Shortfall predictions within a highly accurate statistical threshold).*

## ⚖️ Model Limitations & Assumptions (SR 11-7 Compliance)
As required by standard MRV practices, the following limitations are noted:
1. **Symmetric Tail Dependence:** The Student-t Copula assumes symmetric tail dependence. Market reality dictates that crash correlations (bottom-left) are typically stronger than rally correlations (top-right). Future iterations may explore Skewed-t or Vine Copulas to capture asymmetric dependency structures.
2. **Stationary Regimes:** The Copula correlation matrix is estimated statically over the lookback window, ignoring potential structural breaks or regime shifts.
